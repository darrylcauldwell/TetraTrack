TetraTrack is a multi-discipline training app for tetrathlon and eventing athletes. Apple Watch is the primary session capture device — iPhone is for review, annotation, and training.

LOCATION PERMISSION: Used to record GPS routes during Watch workouts and for live location sharing with family. Background location enables tracking when the screen locks during active exercise.

HEALTHKIT: Watch saves workouts to Apple Health. iPhone reads heart rate, route, and activity metrics for post-session enrichment and insights.

CAMERA: Used to scan shooting targets and mark shot holes for analysis.

MICROPHONE: Used for voice notes during training sessions.

APPLE WATCH: Required for session capture. All five disciplines (riding, running, swimming, walking, shooting) start and record on Watch.

To test the app:
1. Grant permissions when prompted
2. Home screen shows: Training, Schooling, Competitions, Session History, Live Sharing
3. Training: Browse drills by discipline, start a drill for real-time motion feedback
4. Training > Shot Analysis: Scan a shooting target with camera, mark holes
5. Schooling: Browse flatwork, polework, and groundwork exercises
6. Competitions: View calendar, record competition day scores
7. Session History: View completed Watch workouts and drill sessions
8. On Apple Watch: Swipe between 5 discipline pages, tap to start a session
